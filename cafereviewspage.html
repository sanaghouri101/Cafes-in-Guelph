Cafe #1: Cafe Galata - ★★★★☆

A Hidden Gem in the Heart of Downtown

Galata is a Turkish restaurant that has quietly become one of my favorite spots in the city. I'll be honest, I don't know a lot about Turkish food and culture, and walking in for the first time, I wasn't quite sure what to expect. But that uncertainty faded almost immediately. The staff were incredibly warm and welcoming, taking the time to guide me through the menu and make me feel completely at ease. There weren't many people sitting in during my visit, but there was a steady stream of customers coming in and out, picking up orders to go, grabbing something quick. It gave the space an interesting energy, lively enough to feel like a popular spot, but quiet enough that you can actually settle in and breathe.

And settle in I did! I decided on something safe, hot chocolate. It was rich, creamy, and deeply satisfying, it was the kind of drink that feels like a warm hug in a cup. What made it even better? Dipping thick slices of traditional Turkish bread right into it. It sounds simple, but the combination was absolutely delicious.

Galata has become my go-to whenever I'm craving a quiet escape. I've now had their Turkish coffee, tea, and a countless number of their pastries.

Cafe #2: Centurion Coffee - ★★★★★

My Most Visited Coffee Spot

Centurion Coffee holds a special place in my routine, and my origin story as a coffee drinker. As someone who was strictly a milk tea person, their iced caramel macchiato was the drink that converted me, it's that good.

Their prices are refreshingly fair for the quality you get, which is probably part of why the place is always packed. Finding a seat to actually work there is nothing short of a miracle, and I've long accepted that Centurion is a grab-and-go place for me. I pick up my drink and take my laptop elsewhere.

As for the famous salted chocolate chip cookie that everyone seems to rave about, it's good, just not the revelation people make it out to be. I'd grab one if I wanted something sweet alongside my drink, but I wouldn't make a special trip for it.

In short: come for the iced caramel macchiato. Leave quickly, because someone else is already eyeing your table.

Cafe #3:Sol Bakehouse - ★★★★☆

A Trendy Restaurant That Actually Delivers

I'll be honest, Sol Bakehouse wasn't on my radar until it kept popping up on my TikTok feed. After seeing it enough times, I figured I had to see what the hype was about. Safe to say, it was one of the better decisions I've made, and it's earned a permanent spot on my café rotation. I often play it safe and order my go-to iced caramel macchiato, which hit the spot as always. My friend, however, was once more adventurous and went for the blueberry matcha, and I'm not exaggerating when I say it was delicious. Vibrant, refreshing, and just the right balance of flavours. She also picked up a stuffed croissant, which she loved. Between the two of us, everything we tried was great.

The prices are on the higher end, there's no getting around that. But the moment you walk in, it makes sense. Sol Bakehouse is a genuinely beautiful space. Tall windows flood the room with natural light, plants are tucked into every corner, and the open concept layout gives it an airy, relaxed feel. It's the kind of place that looks effortlessly Instagrammable without feeling like it's trying too hard.

It does tend to get packed, which is the one downside. I've still managed to get some solid studying done there, but I'd love to come back on a quieter morning when I can really spread out and enjoy the atmosphere without the crowds. That feels like the ideal Sol Bakehouse experience, as natural light streaming through those big windows, a good drink in hand, and nowhere to be. The location is another major plus. It sits extremely close to Guelph Central Station, making it super easy to pop in whether you're commuting, running errands, or just looking for a reason to get out of the house.

Cafe #4: Cavan Coffee - ★★★★☆

A Unique Cafe with a Passionate Purpose

Cavan Coffee came into my life the way the best recommendations do, through a friend who swore by it. It's the kind of space where you can tell that the people behind it genuinely care about the craft, the beans, and the coffee experience in general. You can feel that the owners really know their stuff when it comes to coffee, and honestly, I expected that to feel a little intimidating walking in as someone who is extremely new to the café scene.

But that intimidation disappeared almost instantly. One of the owner brothers took the time to break down the menu for me, explaining fancy terms, walking me through the flavor profiles, and making the whole thing feel approachable rather than overwhelming. It was genuinely one of the most informative and welcoming experiences I've had at any café.

Out of all four cafés in this list, Cavan easily has the most interesting and creative drink menu. I've already got my next order locked in, I'm eyeing the cinnamon toast latte and the brown butter latte. They also roast their own beans in house, which is impressive on its own and speaks to just how seriously they take what they do.
