Tips and Ettiquette at Cafe's

1. Avoid going to a cafe when you need to do a formal assessment

You can't always rely on the Wi-Fi, and it's not worth the risk. Being in a public place also has a higher chance of distracting you, which is the last thing you want when you are doing a timed assessment.

2. Keep your voice down when on calls

If you need to take a phone or video call, step outside if you can. Nobody around you wants to hear your meeting, and it disrupts the calm atmosphere most people come to a cafe for

3.Don't spread out more than you need to

Please resist the urge to claim the entire table with your laptop, notebook, water bottle, and jacket. Keep your setup compact, especially when the cafe is busy, so others can comfortably share the space.

4. Bring Headphones

Cafes can get noisy, and having headphones is a game changer for focus.

5. Don't save seats for an extended period of time

Dropping your bag on a chair to hold a spot for a friend who's still ten minutes away is fine, but reserving an entire table for a group that hasn't arrived yet can cause annoyance and inconvenience for others.

6. Get to the cafe early if you want to secure a spot

Popular cafes fill up fast, especially on weekends or during exam season. Arriving early gives you the best pick of seating, whether that's a quiet corner, a spot near an outlet, or a seat by the window.

7. Clean up after yourself

When you're ready to leave, take a moment to stack your cups, toss any napkins, and wipe down the table if you made a mess. The staff will appreciate it, and it keeps the space pleasant for the next person sitting down.

8. Try to always buy something

If you're planning to sit and work for a while, make sure you actually purchase something. Cafes are businesses, and occupying a seat without ordering puts pressure on the staff and other customers waiting for a spot.
